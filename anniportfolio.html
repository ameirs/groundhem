Labour Murder


During a workshop in Istanbul lead by the Turkish NGO »Beyond Istanbul«, Anna Greunig and fellow researchers Lila Steinkampf and Insa Deist, engaged in an exchange with the lawyers of the »Marmara Park« trial, thus getting an insight into the court publication of the advocacy which was prepared particularly for this case. As a result of this process, they realized an installation, reconstructing the crime scene in form of architectural renderings and a 3D print model.

On the one hand the translation of evidence into the formal language of an architecture project pitch raises the question, if certain visual strategies can be beneficial to protracted court cases, by using a certain set of visual models. On the other hand it is a commentary on the discrepancy between sold vision and reality inherent in architectural renderings. These images, which usually sell a story of the city as a place of utopian modernity and inconsequential consumption, are now showing the downsides of an urban commercialization, where economic profit is being prioritized over the well being of citizens and workers.

»Labour Murder« is, according to the support group of » Workers’ Families Seeking Justice« (WFSJ), the appropriate term for fatal working accidents which could have been prevented by an adequate inspection of workplaces and the compliance of safety measures. On March 11th, 2012, eleven workers died on the construction site of the Marmara Park Shopping Centre in Istanbul as a result of a fire blaze in their accommodation units. This major project was carried out by the Turkish subsidiary of a German company called ECE Group. The fire was triggered by a short-circuit and highly inflammable nylon tents, which were too close together and had only one exit. By handing over the responsibility to sub-subcontractors, the transnational companies were acquitted. The families of the victims and their lawyers filed a complaint with the Supreme Court, which was rejected.

Virtual Memory


In exchange with the NGO »Hafıza Merkezi«, two web-based projects were developed, which engage with the Kurdish culture of remembrance in Turkey. The first project is a virtual space that deals with the demolition of the Roboski memorial. To defy physical destruction, a 3D Reconstruction of the destroyed monument, was published on virtual-memory.org. In cases of deliberate destruction of cultural sites, the virtual space can be used for reconstruction, thus becoming a space of resistance and counter-narrative. The Roboski monument was erected in Diyarbakır to commemorate 34 civilians of Kurdish origin who were victims of an air strike by the Turkish military in December 2011. During the second project we published, placeholders for »virtual monuments« on Google Maps, dedicated to the civilians who died during the conflict in Diyarbakır in the 1990s. The aim of this experiment was to illustrate the possibility of using Google maps as a tool to place monuments in the virtual space individually.

In the 1990s, clashes between Turkish security forces and the PKK escalated into extreme state violence against Kurdish civilians in form of arbitrary and extrajudicial executions, expulsions and enforced disappearances. The »Hafıza Merkezi/ Truth Justice Memory Center«, founded in Istanbul by a group of human rights lawyers, activists and journalists, has been documenting and publicizing these human rights violations since 2011 and is supporting victims and their families to gain just prosecution and resolution. For decades, Kurdish families have been taking their protest to the streets to demand their rights, commemorate their relatives and raise awareness. The Turkish state, however, systematically suppresses criminal prosecution and reappraisal, which makes the establishment of a culture of remembrance impossible.
