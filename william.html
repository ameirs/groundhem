William Scarfone

is a law student, spatial researcher, and investigative filmmaker. He will graduate in 2023 from the University of Michigan Law School, where he has been awarded a Dean’s Scholarship. Prior to his legal studies, Scarfone completed his undergraduate degree in Film and Electronic Arts at Bard College, was trained in Media and Spatial Practices at Al Quds University in Palestine, and earned his Master’s with Distinction in Forensic Architecture at the Centre For Research Architecture, Goldsmiths, University of London. His research-driven film practice focuses on geopolitical asymmetry and the relationships between armed conflict, media, and the environment. In this capacity, Scarfone has lived and worked in East Jerusalem and the West Bank, Northern Ireland, and North Kurdistan. His work has appeared or been presented at New York University, Gasworks London, Bard’s Difference and Media Project (where he served as a student fellow), and elsewhere. He has worked in a wide range of fields — from the Immersive Media Arts Lab in New York, where he assisted on the realization of an artwork commissioned by the Whitney Museum, to the legal aid offices of the Al Quds Community Action Center in East Jerusalem.
